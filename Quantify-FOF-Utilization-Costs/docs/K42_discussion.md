# Discussion

In this sensitivity analysis using the `FI22_nonperformance_KAAOS` index, higher frailty was strongly associated with increased odds of fear of falling (FOF). The association remained interpretable across FI scaling and corresponded to a 49% increase in the odds of FOF per 0.1 increase in FI. The observed FI distribution and the positive FI-age trend were consistent with expected properties of deficit-accumulation measures, supporting construct validity in this dataset.

The frailty index used here was defined as a sensitivity construct rather than a full-length frailty index. The available KAAOS baseline input was a single structured variable block, so candidate deficits were selected using deterministic eligibility rules with explicit exclusions for performance measures, lifestyle exposures, follow-up variables, and non-health indicators. The resulting `FI22_nonperformance_KAAOS` index therefore represents a conservative baseline non-performance frailty measure with reproducible coding rules and explicit handling of unknown response categories.

The relatively high prevalence of FOF in the analytic sample may reflect cohort characteristics, including older participants with potential mobility limitations or fall risk factors. In addition, the model dataset was restricted to complete cases for FI, FOF, age, and sex, which may have enriched the analytic sample for individuals with higher vulnerability.

The inverse adjusted association between age and FOF should be interpreted in the context of model adjustment. Because frailty captures substantial age-related vulnerability, the residual age coefficient in the adjusted model does not imply a direct protective effect of age.

Strengths of this study include deterministic FI construction rules, run-locked reproducibility, and an auditable artifact trail spanning FI construction, QC outputs, and model summaries. Key limitations include reliance on a single baseline variable block for deficit eligibility and use of a sensitivity FI22 construct rather than a >=30-deficit full-length frailty index.
