# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

Please consider our manuscript, **"Fear of Falling and 12-Month Physical Performance Trajectories: Independent Contributions of Functional Reserve and Deficit Burden"**, for publication in your journal.

This retrospective cohort study evaluates older adults participating in a multifactorial falls prevention program and addresses a clinically relevant question: whether baseline fear of falling is associated with differential 12-month physical performance trajectories, and how reserve and vulnerability constructs contextualize these trajectories.

Our primary models were held constant, and all extended analyses were performed in common analytical samples. We then conducted head-to-head models including both locomotor capacity and frailty index terms, showing moderate correlation but non-collinearity between constructs. Capacity showed a stronger time-dynamic association, while frailty index contributed additional independent information. To test robustness, we performed a covariates-only multiple imputation sensitivity analysis (without follow-up outcome imputation), which restored outcome-complete sample size and preserved direction of key effects.

Methodologically, the manuscript is designed to be transparent and reviewer-safe:

- pre-specified canonical framework preserved;
- construct separation to avoid circularity;
- common-sample comparisons;
- deterministic model-based visualization without refitting;
- explicit non-causal interpretation.

We believe this work will interest readers focused on geriatric function, falls prevention, and frailty operationalization in longitudinal settings.

This manuscript is original, not under consideration elsewhere, and approved by all authors.
