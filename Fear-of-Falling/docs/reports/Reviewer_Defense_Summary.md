# Reviewer Defense Summary

- Pre-specified primary framework preserved. Canonical model specifications were held constant across all extended analyses; capacity and frailty index were introduced only in explicitly labeled extended models.
- Head-to-head comparison in a common analytical sample. All primary and extended models were run on identical samples (`n=236` wide; `n=472` long), preventing differential sample bias in model comparisons.
- No model refitting in visualization. Trajectory figures were derived deterministically from fixed effects of the final BOTH model without refitting or re-estimation.
- Non-overlapping construct definitions. The frailty index excluded performance-based measures and primary exposures (FOF, balance difficulty), preventing circularity.
- Moderate correlation without collinearity. Capacity and frailty index were moderately correlated (`r≈-0.51`) but not collinear, allowing simultaneous modeling without instability.
- Independent longitudinal gradients. In adjusted models, `time×capacity` and `time×FI` retained independent associations with trajectory, supporting non-redundant contributions.
- Missingness robustness. Multiple imputation of baseline covariates (`m=20`) restored the full outcome-complete sample (`n=276` wide; `n=552` long), with no change in sign and only modest attenuation in magnitude.
- Explicitly non-causal interpretation. All findings are framed as descriptive associations; no mechanistic or causal claims are made.
