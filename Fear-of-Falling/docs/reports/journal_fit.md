# Journal Fit (Shortlist)

## Positioning Summary

- Primary clinical question: fear of falling and longitudinal performance trajectories.
- Added explanatory framework: reserve (capacity) and vulnerability (frailty index) as related but non-redundant constructs.
- Study profile: retrospective cohort, longitudinal modeling, falls prevention context, non-causal interpretation.

## Candidate Journals

### 1) BMC Geriatrics

- Fit rationale:
  - Broad geriatric scope with strong acceptance of observational/longitudinal analyses.
  - Good fit for falls, function, frailty, and clinically interpretable methods.
  - Open-access format supports broad clinical visibility.
- Risk:
  - Requires clear practical implications for geriatric care pathways.
- Scope page:
  - https://bmcgeriatr.biomedcentral.com/about

### 2) Journal of the American Medical Directors Association (JAMDA)

- Fit rationale:
  - Focus on clinical aging populations and functional outcomes in care settings.
  - Good thematic alignment with frailty, fall risk, and longitudinal function.
  - Head-to-head reserve/vulnerability framework may be attractive for practice-oriented interpretation.
- Risk:
  - Higher selectivity; discussion must stay tightly clinical.
- Journal page:
  - https://www.jamda.com/

### 3) Age and Ageing

- Fit rationale:
  - High-impact aging journal with interest in trajectories, frailty, and function.
  - Strong methodological transparency in common-sample and sensitivity analyses supports fit.
- Risk:
  - Requires concise contribution statement and high narrative clarity.
- Journal page:
  - https://academic.oup.com/ageing

## Recommended Submission Order

1. Age and Ageing (highest upside, most selective)
2. JAMDA (strong clinical aging fit)
3. BMC Geriatrics (high-probability fit and accessibility)

## Packaging Notes Before Submission

- Keep FOF as the lead clinical narrative in title/abstract/discussion opening.
- Present reserve/vulnerability as explanatory framework, not replacement primary question.
- Keep non-causal wording explicit in abstract, results interpretation, and cover letter.
